what are sea plants

Sea plants are a varied collection of organisms found in salty settings including oceans, seas, and estuaries.Ocean plants range from single-celled creatures to dense kelp forests, having evolved to survive in the aquatic environment. Invertebrates and fish rely on these plants as a major food source, and they also give surrounding marine life oxygen and serve as a haven and nursery for a variety of marine animals.

corols reefs

Coral reefs are amazing undersea ecosystems that are dominated by reef-building corals. Coral reefs are the most diversified marine environment. They are teeming with life, with around one-quarter of all ocean species relying on reefs for food and shelter.

sea grass

Despite the name, seagrass is not a genuine grass. Its nearest terrestrial cousins include lilies and orchids. Seagrasses are flowering plants that grow submerged in marine habitats, usually in shallow coastal waters.

Macroalgae

Macroalgae, or seaweed, are enormous, multicellular marine algae that are classified as plants. They are widely distributed across the world's waters and perform critical roles in marine ecosystems. They are eukaryotic creatures with no vascular tissue (which transports water and other chemicals like as carbohydrates) or organized tissue.

Red algae

Red algae are tiny creatures that may be found in the seas, lakes, rocks, and even on trees.Red algae, also known as Rhodophyta, are a complex category of multicellular marine algae found predominantly in marine habitats, however some species live in freshwater. They are known as "red" algae because of their distinctive reddish hue, which is mostly produced by the presence of pigments like phycoerythrins and phycocyanins.

kelp

Kelp is a large brown algae included in the order Laminariales . It grows in underwater “forests” along temperate and polar coasts worldwide. The massive size, rapid growth rate, and sophisticated roles in marine ecosystems define kelp. In the best situations, some kelp species can attain a height of 150 feet . Kelp forests contribute to ocean flooring ecosystems’ diversity. Kelp grows along coastlines and enjoys cold waters.

Mangroves

Mangroves are distinct ecosystems made up of salt-tolerant trees, bushes, and other flora that thrive along tropical and subtropical coasts in intertidal zones, estuaries, and brackish water conditions. Due to their special adaptations, they can grow in the salty, low-oxygen soil close to the tides.

Marine

Marine algae refer to a variety of photosynthetic organisms found in marine environments like the oceans, seas, and coasts. They serve important ecological purposes, including primary producers, shelters and meals to many marine organisms and play important roles in nutrient cycling and oxygen productionThey are primarily found in aquatic surroundings, most notably the sea, although some can also be found in freshwater.

Green Algae

Green algae, also called Chlorophyta, are a fascinating group of creatures.Green algae are a varied category of photosynthetic organisms, including both marine and freshwater species. Green algae play an important part in the study of plant evolution.

Halophytes

Halophytes are plants that thrive in high-salinity soil or water. They can come into touch with saline water via their roots or by salt spray. Halophytes are plants that have evolved to withstand high salt levels in their habitat. They can be found in salt marshes, salty flats, coastal locations, and inland saline soils.

Acropora coral

Acropora is a genus of tiny polyp stony corals from the Cnidaria phylum. Some of its species include table coral, elkhorn coral, and staghorn coral. Over 149 species have been described. Acropora corals are among the most well-known and ecologically relevant reef-building corals, playing a vital role in the creation and structure of coral reef ecosystems across the world.

sea whip

The sea whip, Leptogorgia virgulata, belongs to the Gorgoniidae family. These corals, like soft corals, have flexible, whip-like branches. Sea whips play a crucial role in coral reef ecosystems, helping to maintain biodiversity, habitat structure, and ecosystem function.

Acropora Coral (Purple Coral)

Acropora is a genus of stony corals with tiny polyps. Acropora is derived from the subclass Zoantharia and the order Scleractinia. Acropora corals are rarely purple; instead, they are usually brown, tan, green, or blue.

Sun corols

Sun corals, or Tubastraea coccinea, are part of the large-polyp stony coral genus. These stony corals generate a rigid skeleton and do not form reefs.This stunning coral originated in the Indo-Pacific, notably the Solomon Islands, the Great Barrier Reef, Tonga, and Fiji.
